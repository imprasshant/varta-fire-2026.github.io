VARTA: Viewpoint Analysis and Representation of Targeted Actors in Indian News (FIRE 2026 Shared Task)
The rapid growth of digital news platforms has significantly increased the influence of media narratives on public opinion, political discourse, and social perceptions. News articles do not merely report events; they often frame individuals, organizations, communities, and institutions in particular ways through selective emphasis, language choices, and narrative structures. Understanding how targeted actors are portrayed and identifying the viewpoints expressed toward them are essential for developing transparent, accountable, and fair media ecosystems. To address this challenge, the VARTA (Viewpoint Analysis and Representation of Targeted Actors in Indian News) shared task at FIRE 2026 aims to advance research in computational media analysis for the Indian news domain.

The task focuses on automatically identifying the viewpoint expressed toward a specific target actor mentioned in a news article and characterizing the nature of its representation. Targeted actors may include politicians, government agencies, social groups, corporations, public institutions, activists, or other entities that play a central role in news reporting. Participants are expected to develop systems capable of detecting whether the portrayal of a target actor is favorable, unfavorable, neutral, or mixed, while also recognizing linguistic and contextual cues that contribute to such representations.
